    Meggan’s Notes

    Air fryers are especially good at sweet potatoes because the circulating heat caramelizes the outside quickly while keeping the centers soft and creamy. You get roasted sweet potato flavor in a fraction of the time without heating the whole oven.

    I usually leave the peel on because it adds texture and saves prep time, but peeling is completely fine if you prefer a softer bite. Either way, the key is cutting the sweet potatoes into evenly sized cubes so they cook at the same rate.

    Why This Recipe Works

    • Texture: A single layer ensures the sweet potatoes roast instead of steam, and the air fryer creates browned edges without long roasting times.
    • Color: Olive oil helps sweet potatoes brown evenly and prevents them from drying out.
    • Flavor: Rosemary, thyme, and garlic complement the natural sweetness of the potatoes.
    • Versatile: The recipe is easy to scale up and cook in batches for meal prep or larger dinners.

    Air Fryer Sweet Potato Ingredients

    At a Glance: Here is a quick snapshot of what ingredients are in this recipe.
    Please see the recipe card below for specific quantities.

    • Sweet potatoes: Choose firm sweet potatoes with smooth skin and no soft spots. Garnet and jewel sweet potatoes both work well here.
    • Olive oil: Helps the potatoes brown evenly and prevents dry spots.
    • Rosemary or thyme: Fresh herbs add savory balance to the sweetness. Either works beautifully.
    • Garlic powder: Adds savory depth without overpowering the herbs.

    How to Make Air Fryer Sweet Potatoes

    1. Preheat air fryer to 400 degrees. In a large bowl, add sweet potatoes, olive oil, rosemary, garlic powder, salt, and pepper (I like at least ¼ teaspoon salt and ⅛ teaspoon pepper). Toss to combine. Be sure to coat the cubes well with oil so they don’t scorch.
    1. Transfer the sweet potatoes to the basket of an air fryer and arrange in a single layer (work in batches if necessary). Leave enough space for air to circulate around the pieces without overcrowding.
    1. Air fry at 400 degrees for 10 to 12 minutes or until the sweet potatoes are tender-crisp with browned edges. Shake the basket halfway through to ensure even heating.

    Air Fryer Sweet Potato Tips and Variations

    • Yield: This recipe makes about 2 cups sweet potatoes, enough for 4 servings, ½ cup each.
    • Cut evenly: Try to keep the cubes around 1 inch so they cook evenly.
    • Crowd control: Leave space between the sweet potatoes so hot air can circulate properly.
    • Shake halfway through: This helps the cubes brown on multiple sides.
    • Leave the peel on: The skin adds texture and saves time during prep.
    • Add spice: Try smoked paprika, Cajun seasoning, barbecue seasoning, chili powder, or a pinch of cayenne for extra flavor.
    • Make them sweeter: Toss with cinnamon and a drizzle of maple syrup after cooking for a sweeter variation.

    How to Store Air Fryer Sweet Potatoes

    • Refrigerator: Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days.
    • Reheat: Warm in the air fryer for a few minutes to help restore the browned edges.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Do I have to peel sweet potatoes before air frying?

    No. The peel adds texture and helps the cubes hold their shape. Simply scrub the sweet potatoes well before cutting.

    Why are my sweet potatoes burning?

    Sweet potatoes contain natural sugars that caramelize quickly. Be sure the cubes are evenly coated with oil and avoid overcrowding the basket.

    Can I use dried herbs instead of fresh?

    Yes. Use about 1 teaspoon dried rosemary or thyme in place of fresh herbs.

    Can I make these ahead of time?

    Yes. They reheat well and are great for meal prep throughout the week.

    Air Fryer Sweet Potatoes

    By Meggan Hill
    These Air Fryer Sweet Potatoes are one of the easiest side dishes to keep on repeat. They cook quickly, pair with almost anything, and turn out beautifully caramelized every time.
    Servings 4 servings (½ cup each)
    Course Side Dish
    Cuisine American
    Calories 161
    No ratings yet
    ReviewPrint

    Ingredients 

    • 1 pound sweet potatoes scrubbed (peel if desired) and cut into 1-inch cubes (see note 1)
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 tablespoon minced fresh rosemary or thyme
    • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
    • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

    Instructions 

    • Preheat air fryer to 400 degrees. In a large bowl, add sweet potatoes, olive oil, rosemary, garlic powder, salt, and pepper (I like at least ¼ teaspoon salt and ⅛ teaspoon pepper). Toss to combine. Be sure to coat the cubes well with oil so they don't scorch.
    • Transfer the sweet potatoes to the basket of an air fryer and arrange in a single layer (work in batches if necessary). Leave enough space for air to circulate around the pieces without overcrowding.
    • Air fry at 400 degrees for 10 to 12 minutes or until the sweet potatoes are tender-crisp with browned edges. Shake the basket halfway through to ensure even heating.

    Notes

    1. Sweet potatoes: To save time, leave the skin on. For a softer bite, peel your sweet potatoes before air frying.
